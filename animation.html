What is Animation?


Animation is a technique for creating the appearance of movement in a series by shooting consecutive drawings, models, or even puppets. Because our eyes can only hold a picture for around a second, When numerous pictures occur in rapid succession, the brain combines them into a single moving vision in a fraction of a second. Pictures are sketched or painted on clear celluloid sheets to be captured in conventional animation. Early cartoons were instances of this, but nowadays, the majority of animated films are created using computer-generated imagery, or CGI.





What is the purpose of Animation?


The most common application of animation is for entertainment. Animation may be found on television, smartphones, and the internet. Animation is commonly utilized to distract youngsters on television since it provides them with something to chuckle about and keeps them interested for lengthy periods of time.
