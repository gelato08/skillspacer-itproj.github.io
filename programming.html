What is Programming?


Programming is a method of instructing a computer to carry out certain tasks. "Instruct the computer" simply means giving it a set of instructions expressed in a language the computer understands. The instructions might be in a variety of formats. Computers, like humans, can understand a limited number of languages (English, Spanish, Mandarin, French, and so on). Computers can read instructions expressed in a programming language, which has a certain syntactical structure.





What is the purpose of Programming?


Programming languages are mostly used to send instructions to computers. Programming languages, in contrast to most other forms of human expression, demand a higher level of precision and completeness. Human writers and speakers can be vague and make slight errors while communicating with others in natural language and still expect their message to be understood. Computers, on the other hand, do precisely what they are taught and cannot comprehend the code that the programmer "intended" to create. The external behavior that occurs when the program is performed must be completely specified by the combination of the language specification, the program, and the program's inputs.
